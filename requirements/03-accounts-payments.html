Doc 3 — Accounts, Payments & Subscriptions

Experiment scope: payments run against a sandbox/mock provider (e.g. Stripe test mode). The flow must be production-shaped so conversion measurement is realistic, but no real money moves.

1. User accounts & identity (US / CIAM)

Architecture: identity is managed by an external CIAM (Customer Identity & Access Management) platform speaking OIDC/OAuth 2 — the Ory stack (Kratos + Hydra; see TS-04). The CIAM owns registration, login, password reset, and credential storage, and issues JWT tokens. The paywall side never handles passwords; it validates JWTs (see NFR-20..24) and maps the token subject to a local user record. In the experiment Ory runs as self-hosted containers (TS-06), swappable for Ory Network in production.
IDPriorityRequirement
US-01MUSTRegistration, login, logout, and password reset are delegated to the CIAM via OIDC Authorization Code flow with PKCE. The paywall never sees or stores credentials. Email verification is enforced by the CIAM before the account is usable (email_verified claim).
US-02MUSTAfter OIDC login the application maintains its session via the CIAM-issued tokens: short-lived access token (≤ 15 min) plus refresh token; "remember me" persistence (default 30 days) is implemented through refresh-token lifetime, not long-lived access tokens.
US-03MUSTA local user record is created/updated on first login, keyed by the immutable CIAM sub claim — local user_id maps 1:1 to ciam_sub. Email is mirrored for display/notifications but the CIAM is the source of truth.
US-04MUSTOn registration or login, the anonymous visitor_id is linked to the user_id and meter state is merged (MT-03); the experiment variant assignment carries over (EX-03).
US-05SHOULDThe CIAM registration form asks only for email and password — every extra field measurably reduces conversion.
US-06COULDSocial login (Google/Apple) enabled as identity providers in the CIAM — no paywall-side changes.
US-07MUSTAccount page where the user can view/cancel their subscription, manage credentials (deep link to CIAM self-service), and delete their account (GDPR) — deletion removes the local record and triggers deletion in the CIAM.
US-08MUSTAll channels (web, app, chat) authenticate against the same CIAM realm, so one identity and one subscription work across channels (API-06).
US-09SHOULDLogout revokes the refresh token at the CIAM and clears the local session; access tokens are left to expire (≤ 15 min exposure).

2. Plans & offers (PAY)

IDPriorityRequirement
PAY-01MUSTTwo tiers — basic (digital articles) and complete (articles + extras such as puzzles/podcasts/e-paper) — each in monthly and annual billing (4 plans total), defined as configuration with price, period, currency, tier rank, and entitlement granted.
PAY-01aMUSTTiers have an explicit rank (basic=1, complete=2) so upsell/downsell logic (UP-*/DN-*) can reason about "higher" and "lower" plans generically.
PAY-02MUSTIntroductory offer support: e.g. first month €1, then full price. Offer shown on the gate must match checkout exactly.
PAY-03MUSTCheckout completes in ≤ 3 steps from gate click: (1) account (skip if logged in), (2) plan + payment details, (3) confirmation.
PAY-04MUSTAfter successful checkout the user is returned to the article that triggered the gate, now unlocked (AC-12).
PAY-05MUSTPayment provider integration is behind an interface so the sandbox provider can be swapped for a real one without touching paywall logic.
PAY-06SHOULDSupport iDEAL and credit card as payment method options in the (sandbox) flow, reflecting the Dutch market.
PAY-07SHOULDFailed payments show a clear retry path without losing entered data.

3. Subscription lifecycle (SUB)

IDPriorityRequirement
SUB-01MUSTA subscription record has: user_id, plan_id, status (active, past_due, canceled, expired), current_period_end, and the entitlement it grants.
SUB-02MUSTStatus transitions are driven by provider webhooks (payment succeeded/failed, subscription canceled) and processed idempotently.
SUB-03MUSTCancellation is effective at period end: status canceled retains access until current_period_end, then becomes expired (AC-02).
SUB-04MUSTOnline self-service cancellation in at most 2 clicks from the account page — no retention call, no dark patterns (legal requirement in NL/EU).
SUB-05SHOULDA past_due grace period of 7 days with access retained, during which payment retry emails are sent (sandbox: logged, not sent).
SUB-06COULDCancel-flow survey (one optional question: reason for leaving) logged for analysis.

4. Upsell & downsell triggering (UP / DN)

Scope: this section specifies triggering and offer-selection logic only. Presentation (banners, modals, offer pages) is a separate shop-like feature and out of scope here. The engine emits an offer object; how it is rendered is decided later.

Architecture: offer decisions come from an external CEP engine (Customer Engagement Platform). The paywall side sends trigger events with full context (including channel: web, app, chat) and enforces/executes the offers the CEP returns. Local code provides the interface, guardrails, and a fallback — not the offer rules themselves.

4.1 Offer engine

IDPriorityRequirement
UP-01MUSTA single entry point decide_offer(subject, trigger_context) → offer | none is the only way callers obtain offers. The trigger context contains at minimum: trigger (checkout, account_view, cancel_intent, payment_failed, gate_shown), channel (web | app | chat), current plan, and variant. At most one offer is returned.
UP-01aMUSTInternally, decide_offer calls the CEP engine with subject + trigger context and returns the CEP's decision after local validation (UP-09). The CEP client sits behind an interface (like PAY-05) with a mock implementation for the experiment.
UP-02MUSTAn offer object contains: offer_id, kind (upsell | downsell | access_grant, see FGA-07), from_plan, to_plan, optional discount (percent or fixed, duration in periods), optional pause (months), valid_until, trigger, channels (channels the offer may be presented on), and source (CEP campaign/rule reference).
UP-02aMUSTAn offer is only returned to a caller whose request channel is in the offer's channels list; otherwise it is suppressed with reason channel_mismatch.
UP-03MUSTOffer rules (trigger → eligibility → offer) live in the CEP engine. Local configuration (API-03) holds only: guardrail parameters (UP-05, DN-05), channel definitions, CEP endpoint/timeout, and an optional static fallback offer per trigger.
UP-04MUSTEvery evaluation is logged as an event (AN-02): offer_triggered with the offer, or offer_suppressed with the reason (ineligible, capped, none matched, channel_mismatch, cep_timeout, cep_error, guardrail_rejected). Both include the channel.
UP-05MUSTFrequency capping: the same subject is not offered the same offer_id again within a configurable cooldown (default 30 days) after declining it. The cap is cross-channel: a decline on app also suppresses the offer on web and chat.
UP-06SHOULDOffers are variant-aware: the variant is passed to the CEP in the trigger context so upsell/downsell strategies are A/B-testable (EX-02) and consistent with the paywall experiment.
UP-07MUSTCEP resilience: if the CEP does not respond within the configured timeout (default 300 ms) or errors, decide_offer returns the static fallback offer if configured, else none. A CEP failure must never block or delay checkout, cancellation, or page render (cf. NFR-11).
UP-08SHOULDCEP-initiated offers: the CEP may push offers for asynchronous channels (e.g. chat, email) referencing a subject_id. These enter through the same validation (UP-09) and logging (UP-04) path before being stored as pending offers for that subject + channel.
UP-09MUSTLocal guardrails: every CEP offer is validated locally before use: plans exist, tier transition is coherent with rank (PAY-01a), discount within configured bounds, frequency caps (UP-05, DN-05) respected, cancellation never blocked (DN-02). Offers failing validation are suppressed with reason guardrail_rejected.

4.2 Upsell triggers

IDPriorityRequirement
UP-10MUSTCheckout, billing period: a subject checking out a monthly plan triggers an annual-plan offer for the same tier (showing the savings).
UP-11MUSTCheckout, tier: a subject checking out basic triggers a complete offer at a configurable add-on price.
UP-12MUSTActive subscriber, tier: a basic subscriber hitting content/features that require complete entitlement triggers a tier-upgrade offer instead of a plain "no access" response.
UP-13SHOULDEngagement-based: a basic monthly subscriber whose engagement score (DY-01 input data) exceeds a configurable threshold for 2 consecutive months triggers an annual or complete offer via account_view context.
UP-14MUSTUpgrade execution: tier or period change is prorated by the payment provider, entitlement updates immediately, and the subscription record keeps its history (SUB-01).

4.3 Downsell triggers

IDPriorityRequirement
DN-01MUSTCancel intent: when a subscriber starts the cancel flow (SUB-04), decide_offer is called with trigger cancel_intent. The CEP campaign is expected to follow the precedence: (1) lower tier (complete→basic), (2) temporary discount (e.g. 50% for 3 months), (3) pause (1–3 months). At most one offer is returned (UP-01) and it must pass local guardrails (UP-09).
DN-02MUSTThe downsell offer never blocks cancellation: declining must complete the cancellation in the same flow, preserving the 2-click guarantee of SUB-04.
DN-03MUSTPayment failure: after the grace period (SUB-05) and before expiry, a payment_failed trigger may offer a cheaper plan instead of losing the subscriber.
DN-04MUSTDowngrade execution: lower tier takes effect at the next billing date (no mid-period loss of paid features); pause sets status paused with access off and billing suspended, auto-resuming after the pause.
DN-05MUSTA subject can accept at most one retention (discount/pause) offer per rolling 12 months — no repeated cancel-to-get-discount farming.
DN-06SHOULDAcceptance and decline of each offer are logged (offer_accepted / offer_declined) with the offer object, so retention value per offer type is measurable (AN-10).

4.4 Subscription status addition

IDPriorityRequirement
SUB-07MUSTstatus (SUB-01) gains the value paused; AC-02 treats paused as no access. Scheduled resume re-activates billing and entitlement.

5. Transactional email (sandbox: log only)

IDPriorityRequirement
US-10SHOULDEmails for: verification, password reset, purchase confirmation, payment failure, cancellation confirmation. In the experiment these are written to a mail log / mailhog instance.
