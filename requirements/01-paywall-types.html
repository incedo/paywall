Doc 1 — Paywall Types & Business Rules

1. Overview of the four models

TypeBehaviorStrengthRisk
HardAll premium content locked for non-subscribers; only teaser visible.Maximum subscriber valueKills reach and SEO traffic
MeteredVisitor reads N premium articles free per month, then the gate appears.Balances reach and conversionEasily bypassed if client-side only
FreemiumEditors mark each article free or premium; premium always gated.Editorial control, predictableWrong free/premium mix hurts either reach or revenue
DynamicEngagement-based score decides per visitor when the gate appears.Highest conversion potentialComplexity; needs data volume

2. Common requirements (all types)

IDPriorityRequirement
PW-01MUSTEvery article has a content_tier field: free or premium. Free articles are never gated by any paywall type.
PW-02MUSTWhen gated, the visitor sees the article headline, lead image, and the first ~150 words (configurable teaser length), followed by the gate component.
PW-03MUSTThe gate shows: value proposition, price of the offer, primary CTA ("Subscribe"), secondary CTA ("Log in" for existing subscribers).
PW-04MUSTActive subscribers with a valid entitlement never see a gate (see AC-*).
PW-05MUSTThe active paywall type for a visitor is determined by their experiment variant assignment (see EX-*), not hard-coded.
PW-06MUSTEach paywall type's parameters (meter size, teaser length, score threshold) are configuration, changeable without redeploying code.
PW-07SHOULDGate copy and price are themselves variant-configurable so messaging can be tested independently of paywall type.
PW-08SHOULDSupport single-purchase passes (day/week) on the gate in addition to subscriptions, implemented as purchasable FGA grants with TTL = pass duration (FGA-*, GAP-05).

3. Hard paywall

IDPriorityRequirement
PW-10MUSTAll premium articles are gated for every visitor without a valid entitlement, regardless of history.
PW-11SHOULDThe gate for hard-paywall visitors emphasizes the full catalogue ("unlimited access") rather than "you've reached your limit".

4. Metered paywall

IDPriorityRequirement
PW-20MUSTA visitor may read meter_limit premium articles per calendar month free (default: 5). Reading article meter_limit + 1 triggers the gate.
PW-21MUSTRe-reading an already-counted article within the same period does not consume an extra meter credit.
PW-22MUSTA meter counter indicator is shown after each counted read ("2 of 5 free articles this month").
PW-23SHOULDWhen one credit remains, show a soft banner nudging registration or subscription before the hard stop.
PW-24SHOULDThe meter resets on the first day of each calendar month (00:00 Europe/Amsterdam).
PW-25COULDRegistered (free-account) visitors get a higher meter limit than anonymous visitors (e.g. 5 vs 3) to incentivize registration.

5. Freemium paywall

IDPriorityRequirement
PW-30MUSTBehavior follows content_tier only: free articles open, premium articles always gated for non-subscribers. No meter.
PW-31MUSTPremium articles are visually labeled (e.g. a "Premium" badge) in lists, search results, and on the article page.
PW-32SHOULDEditors can change an article's tier at any time via the CMS field; the change takes effect within 5 minutes (cache TTL).

6. Dynamic paywall

Scoring logic is specified in Doc 4 (DY-*). Business rules:

IDPriorityRequirement
PW-40MUSTEach premium page view computes/retrieves the visitor's propensity score and compares it to the configured threshold to decide: open, soft gate (dismissible), or hard gate.
PW-41MUSTFor the experiment's first phase, the score MAY be a transparent rule-based heuristic (visit frequency, recency, article count, referrer) rather than a trained model.
PW-42MUSTA floor rule guarantees the gate always appears at or before the Nth premium article in a month (default 10), so no visitor reads unlimited premium content.
PW-43SHOULDThe decision (open/soft/hard + score) is logged on every premium page view for offline analysis.

7. Registration wall (supporting mechanism)

IDPriorityRequirement
PW-50SHOULDAny variant can be configured to interpose a registration wall (free account required) before the meter or gate, to capture identity and email.
PW-51SHOULDAfter registration, the visitor returns to the exact article and position they were reading.
